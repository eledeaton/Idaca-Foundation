Our Mission

At IDACA our mission is to achieve improvements in societies by advocating for quality education, Judicial reform, information ‘richness’, women empowerment, good governance and legal protection.

Scholarships

We will Provide scholarships for students of humble background.

Judicial Reform

We will work hard to Advocate for reforms in the judiciary system so that citizens can get justice

Access to information

We will provide access to information, via the internet, books and printed media so that everyone can be informed and up to date with what's happening in the world.

Woman empowerment

Advocate for an end to early child marriages, domestic violence and forced marriage.

Good governance

Organizing workshops, training sessions and lectures courses on good governance is another area of our focus.

Legal representation

We also provide legal services for citizens who cannot afford the cost, doing so on a pro bono basis.

IDACA Structure

The Board of Trustees sits a top of the management structure, they are tasked with creating and articulating our vision.
The Stakeholders Coordination Council (SCC) works closely along side the board of trustees and comprises of representatives from the government, parents, traditional elders, CSOs, and law enforcing agencies.
The Executive Director of the project is tasked with providing leadership of the project as well as providing technical and financial resources to The Finance & Administration, Resources Mobilization, Legal Affairs, Girls Scholarship and M&E departments.
The Scholarship Unit facilitates the education of girls who have demonstrated wiliness to study but their parents are not able to provide education.
The Mentoring and Evaluation (M&E) department is responsible for tracking progress, improvements and work closely with participants who are facing challenges.

About Us

The Isaiah Diing Abraham Chan Awuol (IDACA) Foundation is a non-for-profit organization that aims to build progressive societies in Africa and the rest of the developing world. IDACA was formed mainly to advocate for progressivism: an idea of progress in which advancements in science, technology, economic development and social organization are vital to the improvement of the human condition.
